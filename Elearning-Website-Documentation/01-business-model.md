# E-Learning Website - Business Model

## 1. Product Idea

The product is an e-learning website for high school students in Egypt and private-course teachers. Students can find school-subject courses, enroll in paid or free learning content, watch recorded lessons, solve quizzes and assignments, track their progress, and prepare for exams.

Teachers can publish and manage courses for their subjects, organize lessons by grade and curriculum, communicate important updates, and track student enrollment and performance.

The platform starts with recorded courses and structured exam preparation. Future expansion may include live sessions, parent accounts, homework correction, mobile apps, question banks, and school partnerships.

## 1.1 Training Context

This project is a product owner training exercise, not a real business launch. The documentation will be written as if it will be handed to a software team, but market assumptions, targets, and validation items are practice assumptions unless confirmed by real research.

## 2. Business Goals

- Build a trusted online learning platform for Egyptian high school students.
- Help students study school subjects with organized private-course content.
- Help private-course teachers reach more students beyond physical classrooms.
- Support education centers that want to sell teacher-led courses online.
- Generate revenue through course sales, bundles, subscriptions, or teacher commission.
- Improve student engagement through progress tracking, quizzes, exam preparation, and reminders.
- Support Arabic-first learning experiences suitable for the Egyptian education market.

## 3. Assumptions

These assumptions must be validated before implementation:

- Egyptian high school students and parents are willing to pay for trusted online private courses.
- Private-course teachers are willing to publish courses online if the platform helps them reach more students and protect their content.
- Thanaweya Amma / Egyptian secondary school subjects have enough demand.
- Students value organized lessons, revision content, quizzes, and exam preparation more than certificates.
- Parents influence or perform payment decisions for many students.
- Mobile-friendly access is critical because many students will use phones.
- Prepaid access codes, course access control, and content protection are core launch needs.
- Teachers will create the initial course content.

## 5. Target Users

### Students

Students use the platform to search for subjects, select teachers, enroll in courses, watch lessons, solve quizzes or assignments, track progress, and prepare for school exams.

Primary student types:

- Secondary 1 students
- Secondary 2 students
- Secondary 3 students / Thanaweya Amma students
- Thanaweya Amma students
- Students needing revision before exams
- Students who cannot regularly attend offline private lessons

### Parents

Parents may not use the learning content daily, but they influence trust and payment decisions. They need confidence that the teacher, course, payment process, and student progress are reliable.

### Teachers

Teachers use the platform to create courses, upload lessons, organize content by grade and subject, manage enrollments, track student activity, and earn revenue.

Primary teacher types:

- Private-course teachers
- Subject-specialist teachers
- Teachers with existing offline students
- Teachers with social media audiences
- Education centers that sell teacher-led courses

### Admins

Admins manage the platform, including students, parents, teachers, courses, subjects, grades, payments, approvals, content quality, reports, support, and system settings.

## 6. Value Proposition

### For Students

- Study anytime from mobile or desktop.
- Find trusted teachers by subject and grade.
- Access recorded lessons without depending only on physical private classes.
- Prepare for exams using structured revision and quizzes.
- Track course progress.
- Access free and paid courses.
- Receive reminders for lessons, quizzes, and new content.

### For Parents

- Pay for courses through a clear and trusted process.
- Know what course their child enrolled in.
- See student progress in MVP.
- Reduce travel and scheduling pressure from offline private lessons.

### For Teachers

- Publish and sell courses online.
- Reach students outside their local area.
- Organize lessons by subject, grade, term, and chapter.
- Track enrollment and student progress.
- Earn revenue through enrollments.
- Reduce manual course access and payment follow-up.

### For Admins / Business Owner

- Manage a focused education marketplace.
- Control course quality and approvals.
- Track revenue and platform performance.
- Build a scalable Egyptian high school education business.

## 7. Revenue Model Options

| Model | Description | Launch Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Teacher course purchase | Students or parents buy courses from each teacher separately | Recommended for MVP |
| Prepaid access codes | Admin generates codes with fixed values, such as 50 EGP or 100 EGP, and students/parents redeem them | Recommended for MVP |
| Subject bundle | Students or parents buy a group of courses for one subject, term, or grade | Out of MVP |
| Subscription | Students pay monthly/termly for course access | Later phase |
| Platform commission | Platform takes percentage from instructor course sales | Recommended for marketplace model |
| Teacher promotion | Teachers pay for featured placement or campaigns | Later phase |
| Education center package | Centers pay for branded teacher/course access | Later phase |
| Certificate fee | Students pay for completion certificates | Not recommended for MVP |

Recommended MVP revenue model:

- Free trial lessons for acquisition.
- Paid course purchase per teacher course.
- Prepaid access codes generated by admin with specific values, for example 50 EGP and 100 EGP.
- Students or parents redeem a code to add balance or unlock paid access, depending on the detailed requirements decision.
- Platform commission on teacher course sales.
- Payment is required in MVP.

## 8. Market Positioning

The platform should be positioned as a focused Egyptian high school learning marketplace.

| Positioning | Meaning | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General e-learning marketplace | Many categories for many users | Too broad and hard to compete |
| Egyptian high school marketplace | Focused on grades, subjects, teachers, and exams in Egypt | Best fit for current scope |
| Teacher-owned course platform | Mainly tools for teachers to sell to their own students | Easier launch but less marketplace value |
| School/center LMS | Built for schools or centers to manage students | Requires stronger institutional features |

Recommended starting position:

Start with Egyptian secondary school subjects and selected trusted teachers. This gives the product a clear identity and makes acquisition easier through teacher reputation and student exam needs.

## 9. MVP Business Scope

The first release should prove that students or parents can buy/enroll in school-subject courses and students can complete lessons successfully.

Recommended MVP includes:

- User registration and login
- Student account
- Parent account
- Teacher account
- Course catalog by secondary year, subject, term, chapter, and teacher
- Course details page
- Free and paid course enrollment
- Prepaid code redemption
- Student dashboard
- Parent progress view
- Video lessons
- Progress tracking
- Basic quizzes
- Teacher course management
- Admin course approval
- Basic revenue and enrollment reports
- Arabic-first interface
- Mobile-friendly web experience
- Courses aligned to the official Egyptian curriculum by term and chapter
- Support for all core secondary school subjects
- Secondary 1, Secondary 2, and Secondary 3 scope
- Video no-download protection

Out of MVP:

- Live classes
- Mobile apps
- Community forums or chat groups
- AI recommendations
- Advanced gamification
- Certificates
- Corporate training portal
- English interface, planned after Arabic launch
- Complex instructor payout automation
- Subject bundles
- Direct online card/wallet payment gateway, unless added later

## 11. Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Not enough quality courses | Learners will not enroll | Start with selected instructors or owned content |
| Low learner trust | Low payment conversion | Add instructor profiles, reviews, previews, certificates |
| Scope becomes too large | Delayed launch | Keep MVP focused |
| Video hosting cost increases | Higher operating cost | Use cloud video provider and monitor usage |
| Poor content protection | Course piracy risk | Use no-download video controls, access rules, and watermarking later |
| Weak differentiation | Hard marketing | Focus on Egyptian secondary school, teacher reputation, and curriculum structure |
| Teacher reluctance to join | Low course supply | Start with teachers who already have online audiences |
| Parent payment concerns | Lower paid conversion | Use trusted payment methods and clear refund/access policies |
| Exam-season pressure | Support and performance issues | Plan infrastructure and support around peak periods |

## 12. Validation Questions

Before moving to detailed requirements, answer these:

| Question | Decision |
| --- | --- |
| Who is the primary first customer? | Students and teachers |
| Which high school grades will launch first? | Secondary 1, Secondary 2, and Secondary 3 |
| Which subjects will launch first? | All core subjects |
| Will courses follow the official Egyptian curriculum by term and chapter? | Yes |
| Will the platform sell courses from private teachers, education centers, or both? | Both private teachers and education centers |
| Is payment required in MVP? | Yes, using prepaid access codes generated with fixed values such as 50 EGP and 100 EGP |
| Will students buy each teacher course separately, or can they buy subject bundles? | Students buy teacher courses separately |
| Should parents have their own accounts in MVP? | Yes |
| What level of parent access is needed in MVP? | Payment and progress view |
| Does the platform need Arabic only at launch? | Arabic at launch, English later |
| Who will create the initial course content? | Teachers |
| What is the main business success target for the first 3 months after launch? | Not applicable because this is a training project |

Open product decisions for later documents:

1. Should prepaid codes add wallet balance, unlock a specific course, or support both?
2. How will codes be distributed to students or parents?
3. Can parents redeem codes for more than one child?
4. What reports does admin need for generated, sold, redeemed, expired, and cancelled codes?
5. Should no-download video protection include watermarking in MVP or later?

## 13. Initial Recommendation

For a first version, build a focused e-learning marketplace for Secondary 1, Secondary 2, and Secondary 3 students in Egypt, parents, private-course teachers, and education centers. Support student registration, parent accounts with payment and progress view, teacher profiles, course discovery by secondary year/subject/teacher, official curriculum structure by term and chapter, prepaid access code redemption, separate teacher-course purchases, lesson viewing with no-download protection, progress tracking, basic quizzes, teacher course management, and admin approval.

This gives the software team a clear MVP while allowing the business to validate real demand before investing in advanced features.

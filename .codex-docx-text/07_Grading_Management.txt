Module 4.7: Grading ManagementBusiness Analysis Document
1. Module Purpose
Grading Management handles the evaluation of student exam submissions, calculation of scores, generation of results, review of grading outcomes, publication of approved results, and auditing of grading changes.
2. Scope
• Grade MCQ questions automatically• Grade True/False questions automatically• Allow teachers to grade Essay questions manually• Calculate final scores and percentages• Determine pass/fail status• Generate and publish results• Modify results with justification• Maintain audit history
• Overall course grade calculation as a weighted average of all exam grades
• Grade summary dashboard for teachers (score distribution, pass rate, averages)
• Integration with Assessment Management (submissions), Student Management (progress), and Reporting & Analytics modules

4. Business Rules
Rule ID
Business Rule
BR-01
Students cannot receive results before exam submission.
BR-02
MCQ and True/False questions shall be graded automatically.
BR-03
Essay questions require teacher grading.
BR-04
Results shall not be generated until grading is completed.
BR-05
Scores cannot exceed the maximum exam score.
BR-06
Students can view only their own results.
BR-07
Teachers can grade only exams belonging to their courses.
BR-08
System shall calculate percentages automatically.
BR-09
Pass/Fail status shall be determined using the exam passing score.
BR-10
All grading actions shall be logged.
BR-11
Generated results shall remain hidden until published by the teacher.
BR-12
Teachers may publish results for an entire exam in a single action.
BR-13
Published results may be modified if a valid reason is provided.
BR-14
All modifications shall be recorded for auditing purposes.
BR-15
Submitted exams create results with PendingGrading status.
BR-16
Results move to Draft after grading completion.
BR-17
Only Published results are visible to students.
BR-18
A parent can only view grade data for students explicitly linked to their account.
BR-19
Parents have read-only access to grade information and cannot perform any grading actions.
